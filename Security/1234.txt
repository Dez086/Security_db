Red Team are the breakers or the ethical hackers,
    their role is to act as Attackers and search for security holes that a
    malicious individual can exploit.
Blue Team are the Defenders.
    Responsible from protective measure within an organization.
Purple Team's are the Defenders.
    Responsible from protective measure within an organization.
Yellow Team are the people that build and design software, systems, and
    integrations that make business more efficient. Their focus is usually on
    requirements, functionality, user experience and back-end performance.
Orange Team's main objective is to inspire Yellow Team to be more security
    conscious, increasing their security awareness by providing education to
    benefit software code and design implementation.
    There should be structured and ongoing engagements between Red and Yellow
    Team for the benefit of Yellow. You want your Orange Team to teach your
    developers to think like attackers.
Green Team consists of ongoing structured interactions between the Blue Team
    and members of the software team (Yellow Team). The ultimate goal is to
    improve code-based and design-based defense capability for detection,
    incident response and data forensics.
White Team members include elements of Compliance, Management, Analysts,
    Logistics and more. These are all-knowing, neutral, third-party individuals
    who set the rules of engagement, organize teams, set strategy, perform risk
    assessments and sets plans and monitors progress. They’re essential for
    every organisation, but they’re seen as “too corporate” for the developers,
    “too demanding” for the defenders and “too much of a killjoy” for the attackers.

Read more on team colors here: https://hackernoon.com/introducing-the-infosec-colour-wheel-blending-developers-with-red-and-blue-security-teams-6437c1a07700

Wireless connections:
    PAN (personal), LAN (local), MAN (metropolitan), WAN (wide-area)

What to do WIFI:
    Change default passwords for devices with a very secure password
    Add MAC filter list and keep a guest account in separate channel and passwd
    Use WPA2 or WPA3(recommended)

What to do Mobile:
    OS updated and update periodicali, updated phone if needed.
    Wifi hotspot (share internet access) with a very secure password and WPA2
    Access password/finger
    Uninstall anything you are not using
    Check apps permissions before and after installing. Remove unwanted:
        Settings / Apps & Notifictions / Permission manager, Special app access
        Use Telegram
    Do not connect to unknown wifi
    Beware of phishing attacks: files, links.

Passwords:
    Use strong passwords, don't reuse, use password manager, maybe two.
    1password.com, KeePass, do not use browser password manager, disable it.
